What AI Needs to Know About Your Practice

Millions of new pages appear online every day, most generated by bots. Google and AI platforms ask these four questions before recommending anyone.

"Does this practice actually exist?"

AI looks for verified signals across multiple platforms: DNS records, directory listings, social profiles, and real human activity. Bots don't pay for email or build interconnected social presences.

"What services do they provide?"

AI needs explicit, structured information about what you treat, the modalities you use, and where you're licensed to practice. Vague websites lead to vague (or zero) recommendations.

"Are they actually good at it?"

Client testimonials help, but professional endorsements from peers and supervisors carry more weight. AI looks for verifiable trust signals distributed across authoritative platforms.

"Can someone easily connect with them?"

A buried contact form or confusing booking flow tells AI that the experience won't be good. Clear calls to action and optimized conversion paths signal a practice that's ready for new clients.
