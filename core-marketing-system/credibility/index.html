Pillar 1 of 4

Credibility

Before Google or AI tools trust your website, they need signs that your practice is real, credentialed, and consistent across the web. This is the foundation everything else builds on.

Part 1

Prove that your practice is real

New pages appear online every day, and many are low-quality or fake. Before Google, Gemini, or ChatGPT can trust your practice, they need proof that you are real, located where you say you are, and described consistently across trusted places.

✉

Domain Email

Recommended, not required

A paid email address on your own domain can make the practice look more established. Google Workspace is one good option, but it is not required for Moonraker to work with your practice.

Setting up Google Workspace itself is not part of our core work. If your email provider supports the right records, we can still help with the website and domain records that matter most.

🔒

Email and Domain Records

Proof that your domain is real and protected

Some email and domain records help show that you own your website domain and that your email is protected from spoofing. Visitors rarely see this, but search systems can use it as part of the trust picture.

These records depend on your email provider. We keep the language practical and configure what is supported.

📍

Directory Listings and Citations

Consistent identity across the web

We build and clean up listings so your practice name, address, phone number, and website are consistent in important places online.

This helps Google confirm that the location and contact details on your website match what the rest of the web says.

What this covers

  • Key local listings built or corrected
  • Major data sources checked where relevant
  • Consistent name, address, phone, and website
  • Regional directory variants for Canadian, UK, Australian, NZ, and South African practices
🔗

Owned Profiles and Links

A connected footprint, not social media management

Google and AI tools can use active practice profiles as proof that your practice is real. We review the profiles you already use, then connect the strongest ones where it helps.

How this works

  • YouTube is created and owned by the client. Moonraker improves the channel after manager access is granted.
  • Quora is created by the client. Moonraker uses access for approved proof content only, not general posting.
  • LinkedIn and Facebook references use business pages, not personal profiles.
  • Other active profiles may be linked only where they support the practice footprint.

We do not create, manage, or post to Facebook, LinkedIn, Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest, X, or Google Business Profile feeds.

🛡

Practice Proof Hub

One place that connects important trust points

Our proof hub connects important details about the practice, such as location, credentials, owned profiles, and approved proof assets.

Think of it as a central place that helps search and AI tools see a clearer picture of who you are, where you practice, and why the practice is legitimate.

Why does this matter?

When someone asks Google or an AI tool for therapist options, your website is only one piece of the picture. These systems also look for outside proof that the practice exists and can be trusted.

Part 2

Prove that you can practice

Once your practice is clearly established online, the next step is showing that you are qualified to provide therapy. Google and AI tools look for clear evidence of professional credentials and authority.

🎓

Professional Credentials

Verifiable proof of licensure and training

We help structure your bio page so license links, certifications, specialized training, and NPI details are easier to find. This helps both visitors and search tools verify your qualifications.

🏫

Education and Training

Linked to verifiable institutions

We include verifiable information about your degrees, the institutions you attended, and specialized training programs, with links back to university pages or accreditation bodies. This helps visitors and search tools confirm your background.

🤝

Professional Associations

Membership in recognized organizations

Memberships in organizations like APA, NASW, AAMFT, or specialty associations help confirm your professional standing. We display these with links to your profiles where they are available.

🎤

Media and Publications

Third-party validation of expertise

If you've been featured in media, published articles, appeared on podcasts, or spoken at events, we showcase these on your bio page. Each outside mention helps prospective clients and search tools understand your expertise.
